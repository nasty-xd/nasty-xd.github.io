Nigiri is a traditional Japanese dish consisting of a portion of sushi rice topped with a little wasabi and topped with various types of fish or seafood. Most commonly, these are fresh slices of fish such as salmon, tuna, eel, or seafood such as shrimp. Nigiri is served in small portions and is often garnished with soy sauce or other seasonings.

rolls

Rolls are popular Japanese dishes that are sushi wrapped in nori seaweed with various fillings. The composition may include fish, seafood, vegetables, avocado, as well as sauces and spices. Rolls can be of different types: maki (with filling inside, rice outside), uramaki (with rice outside), tempura (with fried filling) and others. They are usually served with soy sauce, wasabi and pickled ginger.

sashimi

Sashimi is a Japanese dish consisting of thinly sliced raw fish or seafood served without rice. The most common fish used for sashimi are tuna, salmon, eel, octopus, or shellfish. The dish is usually accompanied by soy sauce, wasabi, and pickled ginger. Sashimi is valued for its freshness and high quality ingredients, which reveal the natural flavor of the fish or seafood.

Salmon sashimi is thinly sliced fresh salmon served without rice. It is a simple and elegant dish that allows you to enjoy the pure taste of the fish.
